Russia has a rich history that has shaped its position in contemporary geopolitics. From the expansion of the Russian Empire to the establishment of the Soviet Union, historical events have profoundly influenced its national identity and political landscape. The Cold War era marked a significant period of rivalry between the East and West, affecting global relations. Today, Russia's geopolitical strategies often reflect its historical experiences, leading to tensions with neighboring countries. The annexation of Crimea in 2014 exemplified Russia's assertiveness in reclaiming its perceived historical territories, raising concerns in Europe and beyond. Understanding Russia's past is essential for comprehending its current foreign policy and relations with Ukraine and other nations.
