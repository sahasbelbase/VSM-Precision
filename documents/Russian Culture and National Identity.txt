Russian culture is deeply intertwined with its national identity, reflecting a rich tapestry of literature, art, and traditions. Notable literary figures such as Leo Tolstoy and Fyodor Dostoevsky have left a lasting legacy, exploring themes of morality, existence, and society. The country's artistic heritage includes iconic ballet, classical music, and visual arts, which serve as symbols of national pride. Russian culture also emphasizes resilience and community, rooted in historical experiences of hardship and triumph. Despite modern challenges, the revival of cultural heritage and promotion of the arts play crucial roles in reinforcing national identity and unity among Russians today.
